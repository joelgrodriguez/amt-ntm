How the nav supports the buyer journey

This is not another menu. It routes three real audiences: new business starters, buyers choosing an NTM machine, and owners who need another machine, training, parts, repair, or support.

1. Learn “What is this?”
1
Learning Center Articles, videos, guides, and FAQs build understanding.
2. Compare options “Which machine type?”
2
Machines Compare roof/wall, gutter, accessories, profiles, and capabilities.
3. Decide / convert “How do I move forward?”
3
Machine page + Resources Configure, quote, finance, download literature, or submit a lead form.
4. Own / grow “How do I get support or expand?”
4
Service Hub + Support Owner answers, service booking, training, repair, parts, and repeat-purchase paths.
Prospective owners Learn → compare machines → configure → quote / financing.
Existing owners Find Service Hub answers → book service → buy parts → request training.
Repeat buyers Jump to machines, specs, financing, and quote paths faster.
Business starters Learn the NTM opportunity, then talk to sales or configure a machine.

Rule of thumb: if content teaches, it goes in Learning Center. If it helps someone choose a machine, it stays with Machines. If it moves them toward quote, financing, or lead capture, it goes in Resources. If they already own a machine and need answers, service, repair, training, or parts, it goes in Service & Support / Service Hub.
