The thesis

Top nav names actions, not content buckets.

Today the site sorts content by what NTM produces — Machines, Resources, Learning Center, Service. Visitors don't think in those buckets. They show up trying to do something: get started, pick a machine, buy one, or get help. We rename the top nav to match those four jobs and route every page underneath into one of them.

Principles

Name the action

Top nav says what the user is doing. “Learning Center” and “Resources” disappear as top-level labels.

Educate before we sell

Don’t push configurator or quote forms before visitors understand the product.

Quizzes are guided entry points

Readiness, machine-fit, and gutter-selection quizzes route unsure visitors instead of dropping them in lists.

Configurator becomes the expert shortcut

Stays in the IA but moves to the end of How to buy — not the front door.

Key takeaway

Four action labels replace four content buckets. Every existing page maps into one of the four. Configurator and Learning Center stay — they just move out of the top nav and into the right action path.
