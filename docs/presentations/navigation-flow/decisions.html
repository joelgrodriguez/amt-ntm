Outcome

What we’re asking you to approve.

Three decisions are on the table. The rest of the work — content, design, build — falls out of those three. None of this changes the live site until stakeholders sign off on the IA.

The three decisions

Decision 01

Adopt action-led top nav

Replace today’s top nav with four action labels: Get started · Choose your machine · How to buy · Get owner support. Demote Learning Center and Resources out of top nav.

Decision 02

Configurator becomes an expert shortcut

Configurator stays in the IA, but moves from front-door behavior (homepage, hero CTA) to an expert shortcut at the end of How to buy. Copy and placement change accordingly.

Decision 03

Quizzes become guided entry points

Three quizzes — readiness, machine-fit, gutter selection — get promoted as the loudest items inside their action paths instead of being buried in resource lists.

What changes on top nav vs footer

Top nav (new)

Get started · Choose your machine · How to buy · Get owner support. Plus a small persistent “Talk to a specialist” CTA.

Removed from top nav

Learning Center, Resources, Machines (as a flat catalog), Service & Support. But: /machines/ and the category landing pages stay live and are surfaced as the anchor inside the Choose flyout — see the mega menu mockup.

Footer / utility nav

Learning Center hub, Knowledge Base, Pricing, About, Careers, Contact, social, legal.

Homepage

Hero routes into the four action paths. Configurator router is demoted from the hero.

Net new pages to build

~9 pages — most connective glue, not new content

Get started

  • Start Here landing · single entry for first-time buyers.
  • Roof vs Gutter explainer · branch the visitor early.
  • Business starter learning track · ordered playlist.
  • First-time buyer playlist · curated articles + videos.

Choose your machine

  • Machine chooser landing page · quiz + comparison + branch.
  • “Which category?” chooser block · 2-click branch.
  • SSQ3 / SSQ II / SSR / SSH comparison table.

How to buy & support

  • “How buying from NTM works” walkthrough.
  • Owner support landing page.
  • Parts request path.
  • Repeat-buyer “Add a machine” lane.

Risks & sequencing

Risk

Cosmetic relabeling

If the IA changes but the mega menu keeps the old catalog shape, none of this lands. The mega menu must reflect the action paths.

Risk

Homepage configurator router

It currently sends the highest-traffic page straight to the bottom of the funnel. Must be changed alongside the new How to buy path or the IA is undermined on day one.

Risk

Parts path

The “Get owner support” label promises parts. Either build a real parts path or scope phase 1 to service/training/manuals and ship parts later.

What we’re asking for next

  • Approval of the three decisions above so we can start design and content work against them.
  • Resourcing for the ~9 net-new pages, prioritized in this order: chooser landing, start here, owner support landing, then the rest.
  • A single owner for the homepage redesign so configurator demotion happens alongside the IA shift, not after.
Key takeaway

Approve the three decisions, fund nine new pages, and assign one owner to the homepage. Everything else in this deck falls out of that.
